Specialized Sports Injury Treatments for Quick Recovery and Peak Performance

Sports injuries can sideline even the most dedicated athletes and active individuals. Whether it’s a sprain, ligament tear, tendon injury, or fracture, effective treatment is crucial to restore function, reduce pain, and prevent long-term damage. Dr. Prasanna Kumar G S offers comprehensive sports injury care tailored to your specific needs, helping you get back in the game faster and stronger.

Our sports injury treatment includes precise diagnosis through physical examination and advanced imaging like MRI and ultrasound. We manage a wide range of injuries affecting the knee, shoulder, ankle, elbow, and other joints with both surgical and non-surgical options.

Common Sports Injuries Treated

  • Anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) and other ligament tears
  • Meniscus injuries and cartilage damage
  • Rotator cuff tears and shoulder instability
  • Tendonitis, tendinopathy, and tendon ruptures
  • Fractures and stress fractures
  • Muscle strains and ligament sprains
  • Dislocations and joint injuries

Treatment Approaches

  • Conservative care including rest, ice, compression, and elevation (RICE)
  • Physical therapy and rehabilitation programs customized for athletes
  • Minimally invasive arthroscopic surgeries for ligament repair and cartilage restoration
  • Platelet-rich plasma (PRP) therapy and other regenerative treatments
  • Surgical repair or reconstruction for severe injuries

Some Common Questions

Have questions you want answers to?

Common surgical sports injuries include ACL tears, PCL tears, meniscal tears, rotator cuff tears, Bankart lesions (shoulder instability), ankle ligament reconstruction, Achilles tendon rupture, and cartilage injuries. Dr. Prasanna in Yeshwanthpur treats all these conditions with arthroscopic and open techniques.

ACL reconstruction uses a tendon graft (typically hamstring or patellar tendon) to replace the torn ligament arthroscopically. The graft is secured in bone tunnels drilled in the femur and tibia. Structured rehabilitation allows athletes to return to sport in 6–9 months.

Rotator cuff tears are repaired arthroscopically by reattaching the tendon to bone using suture anchors. Dr. Prasanna performs partial and full-thickness repairs, allowing return to overhead sports in 4–6 months with physiotherapy.

Yes, cartilage injuries are treated with arthroscopic techniques including microfracture, OATS, and biological options. Dr. Prasanna selects the optimal technique based on lesion size, location, and age.

Return-to-sport timelines vary: ankle ligament repair (3–4 months), meniscus repair (4–6 months), rotator cuff repair (4–6 months), and ACL reconstruction (6–9 months). Dr. Prasanna’s structured rehabilitation ensures safe return when strength and neuromuscular function are restored.

Many sports injuries respond to RICE, physiotherapy, bracing, and PRP injections. Surgery is reserved for complete ligament tears, significant cartilage damage, or failed conservative management.

PRP therapy uses concentrated growth factors from the patient’s blood to accelerate tendon, ligament, and cartilage healing. Dr. Prasanna offers PRP for partial tendon tears, mild cartilage damage, and early arthritis.
