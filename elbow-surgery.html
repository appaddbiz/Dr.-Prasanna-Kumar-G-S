Regain Strength and Motion with Expert Elbow Surgery & Treatments

Elbow pain or limited motion can make even the simplest daily activities—like lifting, typing, or reaching—extremely difficult. At our orthopedic center, Dr. Prasanna Kumar G S offers specialized care for a wide range of elbow conditions. Whether it’s sports injuries, fractures, arthritis, or chronic elbow stiffness, we provide accurate diagnosis and advanced elbow treatments in Bangalore to restore your mobility and quality of life.

The elbow is a complex joint that needs to move smoothly while handling force and stability. Our team uses minimally invasive techniques, joint-preserving strategies, and modern implants to ensure long-lasting results with a faster return to function.

Common Elbow Conditions We Treat

  • Tennis Elbow (Lateral Epicondylitis):

    • Inflammation or degeneration of tendons causing pain on the outer side of the elbow; treated with injections, therapy, or surgery.

  • Golfer’s Elbow (Medial Epicondylitis):

    • Similar to tennis elbow but affects the inner side of the elbow.

  • Elbow Fractures & Dislocations:

    • Surgical fixation for trauma-related injuries to restore joint alignment and function.

  • Elbow Arthritis:

    • For patients with joint damage due to rheumatoid or osteoarthritis; options include joint debridement or total elbow replacement.

  • Stiff Elbow/Contractures:

    • Arthroscopic or open release for elbow stiffness affecting range of motion.

  • Ligament Injuries & Instability:

    • Repair or reconstruction of damaged ligaments, especially in athletes or after trauma.

When Should You See an Elbow Specialist?

You may benefit from seeing an orthopedic elbow specialist if you have:

  • Persistent elbow pain not relieved by rest or medications
  • Weak grip strength or difficulty lifting objects
  • Swelling, clicking, or locking of the elbow joint
  • Post-injury stiffness or limited elbow movement
  • Visible deformity or previous fracture not healing well

Have questions you want answers to?

Elbow conditions requiring surgery include chronic tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis), golfer's elbow, elbow fractures, post-traumatic stiffness, elbow arthritis, ulnar nerve entrapment (cubital tunnel syndrome), ligament instability, and loose bodies. Dr. Prasanna in Yeshwanthpur evaluates all conditions comprehensively.

Tennis elbow surgery is recommended when pain persists for 6–12 months despite physiotherapy, injections, and bracing. The procedure releases or repairs the damaged extensor tendon at the lateral epicondyle. Dr. Prasanna offers both open and arthroscopic techniques for optimal outcomes.

Arthroscopic elbow surgery uses a small camera and miniature instruments inserted through tiny incisions to diagnose and treat conditions like loose bodies, arthritis, impingement, and synovitis. It offers faster recovery, less pain, and minimal scarring compared to open procedures.

Recovery depends on the procedure. Minor arthroscopic procedures allow return to light activities within 2–4 weeks. Complex reconstructions or elbow replacement may take 3–6 months. Dr. Prasanna's structured physiotherapy program at Yeshwanthpur ensures optimal functional recovery.

Cubital tunnel syndrome is compression of the ulnar nerve at the elbow causing tingling, numbness, and weakness in the ring and little fingers. Surgery involves nerve decompression or transposition. Dr. Prasanna in Bangalore performs this procedure with high success rates and minimal recovery time.

Yes, total elbow replacement is available at Dr. Prasanna's clinic in Yeshwanthpur, Bangalore. It is recommended for patients with severe elbow arthritis or irreparable fractures in select cases. The procedure replaces the damaged joint with linked prosthetic components to restore pain-free elbow movement.

Yes, elbow contracture (stiffness) can be treated surgically through arthroscopic or open elbow release. The procedure removes scar tissue, bone spurs, and tight capsule to restore range of motion. Dr. Prasanna specializes in elbow contracture release with excellent functional outcomes.

Surgical treatment for chronic tennis elbow has a success rate of over 85–90% in carefully selected patients who fail conservative management. Most patients experience significant or complete pain relief within 3–6 months after surgery at Dr. Prasanna's clinic in Yeshwanthpur.
