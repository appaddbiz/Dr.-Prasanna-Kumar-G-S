Knee pain caused by arthritis, injury, or joint degeneration can severely limit your ability to walk, climb stairs, or enjoy daily activities. Knee replacement surgery involves removing the damaged bone and cartilage and replacing it with high-grade prosthetic components that replicate natural joint movement. With years of experience in performing successful surgeries, Dr. Prasanna ensures optimal outcomes tailored to each patient’s condition.

Types of Knee Replacement Surgeries Offered

  • Total Knee Replacement (TKR):

    • Ideal for patients with severe arthritis or extensive joint damage. Both the femoral and tibial parts of the knee joint are replaced with artificial implants to restore full joint function.

  • Partial Knee Replacement (PKR):

    • Suitable for cases where damage is limited to one compartment of the knee. This minimally invasive option preserves healthy bone and ligaments, leading to faster recovery and natural knee movement.

  • Robotic-Assisted Knee Replacement:

    • A high-precision technique that uses robotic systems for accurate implant placement. This method ensures reduced pain, smaller incisions, and quicker post-op rehabilitation.

  • Revision Knee Replacement:

    • For patients who have had previous knee replacement surgeries but are now facing implant failure, infection, or instability. Dr. Prasanna performs complex revision surgeries with careful planning and advanced techniques.

When Should You Consider Knee Replacement?

You may be a candidate for knee replacement surgery if you experience:

  • Chronic knee pain that persists despite medications or physiotherapy
  • Stiffness, swelling, or inability to bend or straighten the knee
  • Difficulty walking, standing, or climbing stairs
  • Deformity or joint instability
  • X-ray/MRI evidence of cartilage loss or joint degeneration
