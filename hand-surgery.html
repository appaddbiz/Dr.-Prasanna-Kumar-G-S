Expert Hand Surgery and Treatments for Improved Function and Comfort

Your hands are essential for nearly every daily activity—from writing and cooking to lifting, gripping, or using a smartphone. Injuries, repetitive strain, or degenerative conditions of the hand and wrist can severely impact your quality of life. At our orthopedic center, Dr. Prasanna Kumar G S offers specialized hand surgery and treatments in Bangalore for a wide range of issues, combining expert surgical skills with a compassionate approach to care.

We treat traumatic injuries, sports-related conditions, nerve entrapments, arthritis, and congenital deformities affecting the hand, fingers, wrist, and forearm. Our goal is to relieve pain, restore full hand function, and improve your ability to perform everyday tasks confidently.

Common Hand and Wrist Conditions We Treat

  • Fractures & Dislocations:

    • Treatment of hand, finger, and wrist fractures using internal fixation or casting for optimal healing.

  • Carpal Tunnel Syndrome:

    • Minimally invasive decompression surgery to relieve pressure on the median nerve.

  • Trigger Finger:

    • Surgical or injection-based treatment to relieve finger locking or snapping.

  • De Quervain’s Tenosynovitis:

    • Surgical release of inflamed tendons near the thumb and wrist.

  • Arthritis of the Hand or Wrist:

    • Joint debridement, fusion, or replacement in severe cases of osteoarthritis or rheumatoid arthritis.

  • Ganglion Cysts or Tumors:

    • Safe and effective removal of benign masses from the hand or wrist.

  • Congenital Hand Deformities:

    • Surgical correction for children with birth-related hand abnormalities.

When Should You See a Hand Specialist?

You should consult an orthopedic surgeon if you experience:

  • Numbness, tingling, or weakness in the hand or wrist
  • Difficulty gripping, pinching, or performing fine motor tasks
  • Hand or wrist swelling, deformity, or pain after an injury
  • Chronic stiffness, inflammation, or limited hand movement
  • Visible lumps or cysts on the hand or wrist

Some Common Questions

Have questions you want answers to?

Common hand surgical conditions include carpal tunnel syndrome, trigger finger (stenosing tenosynovitis), Dupuytren's contracture, ganglion cysts, hand and finger fractures, flexor/extensor tendon injuries, and basal thumb arthritis. Dr. Prasanna in Yeshwanthpur provides surgical care for all these conditions.

Carpal tunnel release surgery cuts the transverse carpal ligament to relieve pressure on the median nerve, eliminating numbness, tingling, and weakness in the hand. It can be performed as open or endoscopic (minimally invasive) surgery. Dr. Prasanna in Bangalore achieves excellent results with quick recovery.

Trigger finger surgery releases the constricted pulley (A1 pulley) that causes a finger to lock or click during movement. It is typically performed under local anesthesia as an outpatient procedure. Recovery is rapid, with full finger movement restored within 2–4 weeks at Dr. Prasanna's clinic.

Displaced or unstable hand and finger fractures are repaired with K-wires, plates, or screws under image guidance to restore proper bone alignment. Dr. Prasanna in Yeshwanthpur uses minimally invasive fixation techniques to reduce post-operative stiffness and optimize recovery of hand function.

Dupuytren's contracture causes thick fibrous bands to form under the palm skin, pulling one or more fingers into permanent flexion. Surgical fasciectomy removes the diseased tissue and straightens the affected fingers. Dr. Prasanna in Bangalore offers effective surgical and minimally invasive treatment options.

Recovery varies by procedure. Minor surgeries like trigger finger release allow return to normal activities within 2–4 weeks. Complex tendon repairs and fracture fixations may need 6–12 weeks of hand physiotherapy for full grip strength and range of motion restoration at Dr. Prasanna's Yeshwanthpur clinic.

Yes, most hand surgeries including carpal tunnel release, trigger finger release, and ganglion removal are performed as day-care outpatient procedures at Dr. Prasanna's Yeshwanthpur clinic. Patients return home the same day, minimizing disruption to daily life.

Basal thumb (CMC) arthritis causes pain and weakness at the base of the thumb due to wear of the trapezio-metacarpal joint. Surgical options include joint reconstruction (ligament arthroplasty) or joint replacement. Dr. Prasanna in Yeshwanthpur provides tailored surgical solutions for thumb arthritis based on severity and patient needs.
