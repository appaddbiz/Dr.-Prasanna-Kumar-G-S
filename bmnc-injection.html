BMNC Injection Therapy for Joint Preservation and Pain Relief

Bone Marrow-Derived Mononuclear Cell (BMNC) Injection is an advanced regenerative therapy that uses the body’s own healing potential to treat orthopedic conditions such as osteoarthritis, cartilage defects, and joint degeneration. Dr. Prasanna Kumar G S offers BMNC therapy as a cutting-edge, non-surgical treatment option aimed at pain reduction and joint preservation.

The procedure involves harvesting bone marrow, isolating mononuclear cells, and injecting them precisely into the affected joint or tissue. These cells release growth factors and aid in tissue repair, inflammation control, and cartilage regeneration. It is a promising solution for patients looking to delay or avoid joint replacement surgery.

Conditions Treated with BMNC Therapy

  • Early to moderate knee osteoarthritis
  • Cartilage wear and tear
  • Avascular necrosis (AVN) of the hip
  • Chronic joint pain and inflammation
  • Mild to moderate degenerative joint disease

Procedure Overview

  • Outpatient or day-care procedure under local anesthesia
  • Bone marrow aspiration from the pelvic bone
  • Isolation of mononuclear cells using specialized processing
  • Image-guided injection into the affected joint or site
  • Post-procedure rest and gradual return to activity

Benefits of BMNC Injection

BMNC therapy is gaining popularity for its regenerative benefits:

  • Minimally invasive and non-surgical
  • Utilizes the patient’s own cells – safe and biocompatible
  • Helps reduce pain and improve joint function
  • May delay or avoid the need for joint replacement
  • Faster recovery compared to surgery
